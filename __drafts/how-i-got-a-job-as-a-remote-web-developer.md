In February of this year I accepted a job offer to work at SpiderOak as their new web developer. This ended a long, frustrating, and altogether weird period of job hunting that started 5 months earlier. During that time I met some good people, was lied to, was left hanging, declined offers, negotiated salaries, and finally got a new job. I learned much from the experience and want to share it with you, whoever you are.

I was sitting on a hotel room sofa outside of Fredericksburg, TX on a trip to that town's annual Oktoberfest celebration. I had taken a few days of vacation from my current job, but we were a tiny startup so that meant I was on the sofa checking on our stack and sweating the fact that stuff was broken; really broken. Sitting next to me, in a portable crib we bought a few weeks ago, was my 2-month old son. As I remember, it was then that I decided my current job wasn't working.

Some things just don't mix well together. Working for a tiny startup with a newborn baby is a good example. So I decided to start looking for a new job. That week on vacation I sent out my first round of cover letters.
