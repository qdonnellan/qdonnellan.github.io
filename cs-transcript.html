CS Transcript

On May 1st, 2017, I made a commitment to myself to work through a series of online computer science courses in an effort to prepare for admission for Georgia Tech's M.S. in Computer Science program. The list of courses I've taken since then is below in the order that I've taken them. In general, here are the principles I've followed for deciding which courses to complete:

  • The course should be graded
  • ... should contain substantial programming assignments
  • ... should yield a verifiable certificate of completion

Additionally, I've decided not to take any Udacity courses that are already within the Georgia Tech curriculum, even though they are free and open for auditing - several current OMSCS students have suggested to me personally that the best experience in the program is achieved when you are exposed for the first time to the material along with all other classmates.

Finally, since I originally made the commitment to begin this coursework, Coursera has introduced a $49 monthly subscription. With this subscription you can take as many concurrent courses as you'd like, so I've been trying to maximize the value of that as much as possible. As a result, most of the courses below are hosted on Coursera.
